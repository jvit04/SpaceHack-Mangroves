Risk from Compacted Soil

The linear park features introduced species with shallow root systems. This causes soil compaction, limiting rainwater infiltration (20 mm/h). The result is a high risk of flooding in surrounding areas during high tides or heavy rainfall.

Aquatic Phase: Bioremediating Floating Islands

Nature-based technology for water quality recovery in the Salado Estuary (Av. Barcelona).

📍

Visibility

Located opposite the Linear Park and Av. Barcelona, acting as visible environmental education tools for citizens.

🚤

Tidal Adaptation

Unlike the shoreline, the islands rise and fall with the estuary's tide, ensuring roots remain in constant contact with the water.

🐦

Biological Corridor

This chain of wetlands reconnects habitat fragments, attracting endemic birdlife (herons, cormorants) and aquatic life.

🌎

Ecological Functions of the Floating Mangrove

Habitat restoration and biodiversity support backed by scientific literature.

✔

Self-sustainability: Re-establish natural assemblage structures and ecosystem functions, supporting self-sustaining coastal ecosystems.

✔

Physical Protection: Attenuate wave energy, reduce erosion, and enhance resilience to sea-level rise and storm surges.

✔

Wildlife Refuge: Provide nursery, feeding, and spawning grounds for diverse aquatic fauna (fish, crustaceans, birds), supporting local biodiversity.

✔

Ecological Corridors: Serve as stepping stones for macrofauna and microorganisms, enhancing ecosystem rehabilitation.

✔

Trophic Resources: Offer complex vegetation structures and food resources, vital for maintaining the estuarine food web.

✔

Blue Carbon: Enhance local livelihoods and contribute significantly to blue carbon sequestration, key in tropical communities.
